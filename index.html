About Me

I'm Sai Peram, a current undergraduate student majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering and minoring in Robotics at UT Austin. I am passionate about robotics and AI technologies to boost real world applications. My work spans autonomous vehicles, unmanned aerial vehicles (drones), and surgical robotics.

Professional Experience

  • As part of The Advanced Robotic Technologies for Surgery Lab (ARTS) at Texas Robotics, I am researching how to boost physical AI systems for precise surgical procedures.
  • Through my work at NOVA: Self-Driving Research Lab, I have worked on autonomous vehicle navigation and multimodal sensor fusion systems to boost the safety of self-driving vehicles. I have experience with Computer Vision models like YOLOv8 for brake light detection and time series forecasting for dynamic occupancy grids.
  • As a Swarm Robotics Engineer at Texas Aerial Robotics, I am applying Multi-Agent Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (MADDPG) to safely control a group of unmanned drones in avoiding collisions with other drones and obstacles.
